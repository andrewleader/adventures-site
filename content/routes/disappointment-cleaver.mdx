The most popular route to the summit of Mount Rainier, offering a challenging but non-technical climb to Washington's highest peak.

## Route Description

This classic mountaineering route begins at Paradise and follows the Muir Snowfield to Camp Muir at 10,188 feet. From there, climbers navigate the Disappointment Cleaver, a rocky ridge that avoids the more technical ice climbing.

## Best Season

Late May through September, with July and August offering the most stable conditions.

## Required Gear

- Mountaineering boots and crampons
- Ice axe and helmet
- Rope and glacier rescue equipment
- Overnight gear for Camp Muir

## Permits

Climbing permits are required and should be reserved well in advance during peak season.
